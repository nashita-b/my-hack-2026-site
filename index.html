About MY-HACK

Empowering Muslim youth through innovation and impact.

Welcome to MY Hack, a hackathon where high school and college students come together to build technological solutions that uplift the Muslim community. Organized by the Mafiq Foundation, this event fosters creativity, learning, and spiritual empowerment through technology.

MY Hackathon is not just a tech event — it's a transformative journey for Muslim youth to create real-world solutions that address urgent social, cultural, and ethical challenges facing our communities today. Designed as a weekend-long innovation sprint, this hackathon brings together high school and college students across the DMV (DC, Maryland, Virginia) area to harness the power of AI, data, design, and community-driven creativity.

Theme: Innovate with Purpose

Faith, Technology, and Social Impact — young Muslims using technology to solve real challenges facing our communities and the Ummah.

Project tracks

Three tracks to build for.

These are starting points, not requirements — bring your own idea if it fits the theme. Every project should connect back to a real challenge facing our communities.

01

Build trust. Strengthen the community.

Challenge

Youth feel disconnected from mosques due to weak governance and conflict — and families are increasingly targeted by scams, impersonation, and cyber threats.

Goal

Tools that strengthen governance and transparency, and privacy-protecting tools that help communities prevent and respond to safety threats.

02

Seek truth. Navigate tech responsibly.

Challenge

Misinformation, manipulated media, unreliable religious content, and AI-generated falsehoods.

Goal

Tools that help people verify claims, evaluate sources, and find reliable information.

03

Serve with purpose. Sustain our communities.

Challenge

Youth want to turn knowledge into action, and mosques often lack simple tools for managing waste, energy, and resources.

Goal

Connect students to real service opportunities, and build tools that help mosques operate more sustainably.

Questions

FAQ

Do I need to have a team before the hackathon starts?

No problem, there's a team formation session to help you find one that day.

Do I need coding experience?

No. You can contribute through design, research, business, writing, public speaking, project management, or problem-solving — teams need every kind of skill, not just engineers.

Do I need to bring a project idea?

No. Suggested tracks are above, but you're also welcome to bring your own idea based on a real challenge in your community.

Does my project need to be fully finished?

No. A functional prototype, mockup, or clear demonstration of the idea is acceptable.

What should I bring?

A laptop and charger are the essentials. Also bring your phone charger, a blanket and small pillow for overnight rest, toiletries if you're staying through the night, a reusable water bottle, and any personal medication. Meals are provided, so pack light.

Do I have to stay overnight, and where do we sleep?

Staying isn't required, but it's encouraged so your team can make the most of the full event. MY-HACK runs on a "no-sleep" model — you can take short naps in separate gender halls, or go home and come back.

Will food be provided?

Yes — meals and snacks are included throughout, along with scheduled prayer breaks.

Will transportation be provided?

Limited support may be available if requested in advance — reach out to the organizers ahead of the event.
